# KICKR Gears - Help Guide

## What is KICKR Gears?

KICKR Gears displays your WAHOO KICKR bike's current gear selection as a floating HUD overlay. Perfect for use with Zwift or other cycling apps where you want to see which gear you're in without looking down at your bike.

## Getting Started

### First Launch
1. Make sure your KICKR bike is powered on
2. Launch KICKR Gears
3. The app will automatically scan and connect to your KICKR
4. Once connected, you'll see your current front and rear gears displayed

### Understanding the Display

**Front Gears (Top):**
- Shows current front chainring (1, 2, or 3)
- Three vertical bars represent your chainrings
- Selected gear is highlighted in white

**Rear Gears (Bottom):**
- Shows current rear cog (01-13)
- Thirteen vertical bars represent your cassette
- Selected gear is highlighted in white
- Bars decrease in height from left to right (gear 1 = tallest, gear 13 = shortest)

## Using with Zwift

1. **Launch Zwift first** (don't use fullscreen mode - use windowed mode)
2. **Launch KICKR Gears**
3. **Right-click** the KICKR Gears window
4. Select **"Unlock Position"**
5. **Drag** the window to position it over your Zwift interface
6. **Right-click** again and select **"Lock Position"**

The window will now stay in place and show over Zwift!

## Settings

Right-click the window and select **"Settings..."** to access:

### Appearance
- **Scale** (60%-160%): Make the display bigger or smaller
- **Transparency** (0%-100%): Adjust background opacity

### Window
- **Enable Dragging**: Unlock/lock window position
- **Reset Position**: Move window back to center of screen

## Troubleshooting

### "No KICKR found"
- Make sure your KICKR bike is powered on
- Check if another app (like Wahoo Fitness) is connected to the bike
- Only one app can connect via Bluetooth at a time
- Click **"Retry"** to scan again

### Window keeps moving
- Right-click the window
- Select **"Lock Position"**

### Gears not updating
- Check connection in Settings
- Make sure the KICKR is paired and connected
- Try disconnecting and reconnecting

### Display too small/large
- Open **Settings → General**
- Adjust the **Scale** slider
- Changes apply immediately

## System Requirements

- macOS 13.0 (Ventura) or later
- Bluetooth enabled
- WAHOO KICKR bike with electronic shifting

## Privacy & Permissions

KICKR Gears requires:
- **Bluetooth permission** to connect to your KICKR bike
- No internet connection needed
- No data is collected or transmitted

## Tips

- **Use windowed mode** in Zwift, not fullscreen
- **Position at top or bottom** of screen where it won't block important info
- **Lower transparency** (higher opacity) if screen content makes it hard to read
- **Lock position** when you've found the perfect spot
- The window is **always on top** so it stays visible

## Keyboard Shortcuts

When the window is focused:
- **⌘ ,** - Open Settings
- **⌘ Q** - Quit app

Right-click menu:
- Quick access to lock/unlock
- Settings
- Quit

## Technical Notes

- Connects via Bluetooth LE service `A026EE0D`
- Compatible with KICKR bikes that report gear data
- Window position and preferences are saved automatically
- Very lightweight - minimal CPU and memory usage
